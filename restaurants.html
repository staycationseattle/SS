RESTAURANTS

Pike Place Chowder

Pike Place Chowder is a Local seafood institution serving traditional

and specialty chowders in a ounter-serve setting Customers rave

about the award winning seafood and chowder. This charming

chower joint is always packed with people so expect a line.

Staycation Seattle rates Pike Place Chowder at 4.5/5.

It really is worth it!

Address: 1530 Post Alley, Seattle, WA 98101

When to visit: Pike Place Chowder is a great place to stop on your

day off or anytime you have some extra time to enjoy the city.

Their famous chowder could also be good on a chilly Fall day in Seattle.

The Pink Door

Owner Jackie Roberts offers an unpretentious escape from

the everyday with a classic, produce-driven menu,

a convivial atmosphere fueled by her warm, friendly

staff (her “GOODPEOPLE”), and ,entertainment such as

trapeze, cabaret, music and tarot. Whether nestled

inside the dining room,relaxing in the lounge or

outside on the view deck, overlooking Elliot Bay,

the mysterious alchemy of The Pink Door’s food,

ambience and service create a habit-forming experience.

Address: 1919 Post Alley, Seattle, WA 98101

When to visit: The Pink Door is a good spot for adults who are looking to spice up their eating experince.

Dinner at the Pink Door is not complete without one of the many shows they put on.

Serious Pie

The Seattle Serious Pie, pizzeria with a bread baker's soul,

serves up pies with blistered crusts,light textured but

with just enough structure and bite. Our attentiveness

to each pizza in the 600 degree stone-encased apple

burning oven preserves the character of house

made charcuterie and artisan cheeses from around the world.

Address: 316 Virginia St, Seattle, WA 98101

When to visit: If you are in the mood for great pizza

and a welcoming enviorment then Serious Pie is

the perfect place for you!

Din Tai Fung

Din Tai Fung was founded in Taipei, Taiwan in 1958 as a cooking

oil retail shop. In 1972, it transitioned into a full fledged

restaurant specializing in soup dumplings and noodles. The

restaurant was soon winning rave reviews from all over

the world.The tradition of Din Tai Fung continues today

in its locations in the United States, Taiwan, Japan,

Singapore, South Korea, Australia, Indonesia, Malaysia,

China, and Thailand.

Address: 600 Pine St #403, Seattle, WA 98101

When to visit: You will find dumplings galore

and a good time at Din Tai Fung

Ristorante Picolinos

Romantic Italian eatery with artisanal wood- and ironwork

plus garden seating and a bakery-espresso bar. Customers

give reviews like "Amazing food" and, "when I go there

I feel at home". Ristorante Picolinos is on the more

expensive side but it is worth it! Staycation Seattle

rates Picolinos at 4/5.

Address: 6415 32nd Ave NW, Seattle, WA 98107

When to visit: When you are in the mood for amazing

Italian food and an attentive wait staff Ristorante

Picolinos is the place for you.
