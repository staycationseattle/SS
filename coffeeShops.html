COFFEE SHOPS

Original Starbucks

This corner store is at the gateway to Seattle’s historic Pike Place

Market – a location of rich history and vital importance to both

Starbucks and the Emerald City. It was, after all, in the Market

that the original Starbucks opened in 1971. The design of our 1st and

Pike coffeehouse evokes the warm heritage of that first store through

its hardwoods, furniture and lighting. The store is LEED® Gold

certified During construction, we made sure to recycle as much of the

construction waste as possible.

Address: 102 Pike St., Seattle, Washington 98101.

When to visit: Anytime! This coffee shop is perfect

for any weather and in any season.

Moore Coffee

Moore Coffee is a quaint family-owned coffee shop located near

Westlake Center in Seattle. It gives off a loungey vibe where you

can get lunch and dinner along with your cup of joe. Customers

give high remarks to the baristas and their intruiging latte art.

Staycation Seattle rates Moore Coffee at 4.5/5 stars.

Address: 1930 2nd Ave, Seattle, WA 98101

When to visit: When you are in the mood for attentive baristas and

inventive latte art that will make your day!

Espresso Vivace

Espresso Vivace is a Seattle area coffee shop and roaster known

for its coffee and roasting practices. Vivace's owner, David Schomer,

is credited with developing and popularizing latte art in the United

States. Espresso Vivace has 3 locations in the Seattle Area. Customres

rate the shop at 5 out of 5 stars due to the latte art and the late

closing time for those late night caffine runs

Address: 227 Yale Ave, Seattle, WA 98109

When to visit: Staycation Seattle belives that summer is the best

time to visit Espresso Vivace becuase of the ample sunlight and the

large windows that let in a nice breeze.

Monorail Espresso

Monorail Espresso is a coffeehouse in Seattle. It is notable as having

been founded as the first espresso cart in the world. On December 1,

1980, Seattle proprietor Chuck Beek set up an espresso cart at

Westlake Center under the Seattle as an experiment to see whether

there was a market for espresso sold on the street rather than in a

traditional coffeehouse. The business model proved successful and in

1997, the business moved from a cart to a walkup window.

Address: 510 Pike St, Seattle, WA 98101 and 701 Fifth Avenue,

Seattle, WA 98104.

When to visit: When you are in the mood for hand-crafted drinks and

delicious cookies.
